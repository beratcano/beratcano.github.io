Pattern

The "X vs Y" comparisons examiners love

PairKey distinction
Master vs transactional dataStable, shared (customer/material) vs daily ops (order/invoice). No good transactions without good master data.
FI vs COExternal/statutory/GAAP-IFRS/closed vs internal/management/flexible.
Cost vs profit center vs internal orderCosts only / revenue+costs / time-limited activity (settled then closed).
Standard vs moving average priceFixed + variance analysis (finished goods) vs recalculated each GR (raw/traded).
Primary vs secondary wage typeEntered (1000–9999) vs system-generated (/100–/999).
IM vs WMPlant/location totals (no bins) vs bin-level with transfer orders.
PR vs POInternal request (no FI, CO commitment) vs legally binding vendor commitment (FI on GR/IR).
Static vs dynamic credit checkCurrent exposure vs exposure + future-horizon orders.
Config vs customization vs modificationParameters / BAdIs & exits / core-code change (avoid).
Vanilla vs custom ERPCheaper, upgrade-safe, best practice vs preserves processes but technical debt + lock-in.
Centralized vs decentralized vs federated MDMOne repository / own data+sync / local data + shared rules.
Data Owner vs StewardAccountable manager (policy/outcomes) vs operational business expert (daily quality).

All the mnemonics

4 core ERP principles

I-S-S-C: Integration, Standard process, Single source, Control/reporting.

6 data-quality dimensions

A-C-C-T-U-V: Accuracy, Completeness, Consistency, Timeliness, Uniqueness, Validity.

G/L ranges

1-2-3-4-5 = A-L-E-R-E: Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenue, Expense.

SAP Activate phases

D-P-E-R-D-R: Discover, Prepare, Explore, Realize, Deploy, Run.

Production order status

CRTD → REL → PCNF → CNF → DLV → TECO → CLSD.

SCOR / ARIS

SCOR = Plan-Source-Make-Deliver-Return-Enable. ARIS = 5 views (Org, Data, Function, Output, Control).

Three-way match

PO + GR + Invoice. (And O2C "601 = COGS"; P2P "FI fires on GR".)

Golden rule

Fit the process to the system, not the system to the process.
