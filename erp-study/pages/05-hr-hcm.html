Human Resources & Payroll — HCM

The people platform behind every business process — from employee record creation to paycheck, stored in time-stamped infotypes and paid through a schema-driven payroll.

What is SAP HCM?

SAP Human Capital Management manages the entire employee lifecycle — from hire to retire: master data & org structure, time/attendance/absence, payroll & legal reporting, and talent (recruiting, performance, learning).

SAP HCM (on-premise)

Legacy ECC / S/4HANA installed on company servers. Deep integration with FI, CO, MM. Still widely used.

SAP SuccessFactors (cloud / SaaS)

SAP's modern cloud HCM platform. Modules: Employee Central, Recruiting, Learning, Performance. Preferred for new implementations since 2015.

Discussion point: a company may keep payroll on-premise (local legal complexity, deep FI/CO links) but run performance/talent in the cloud.

Most critical infotype

IT0001 (Org Assignment) is the most important because it links the employee to the FI cost center. Change it (department transfer) and the next payroll run automatically routes that salary to the new cost center — no manual journal entry.

Time dependency

Every infotype record has a validity period (start → end). SAP keeps the full history — nothing is overwritten, only new records added. This is what enables retroactive payroll corrections.

Time Management

A Work Schedule Rule (WSR) combines: Daily Work Schedule (hours in a day) + Period Work Schedule (weekly pattern) + assignment to a calendar year with public holidays.

Time Evaluation PT60

The bridge to payroll: processes raw time data (clock-in/out, absences, attendances) into payroll-relevant results — overtime → wage types, absence quota deductions, flextime balances, shift premiums. Accurate time data is a prerequisite for accurate payroll.

Critical distinction

Customer wage types start with a number (1000–9999) and are entered. SAP technical/secondary wage types start with a slash (/100–/999) and are system-generated — never enter them manually.

Talent management & integration

Recruitment → vacancy → posting → screening → interview → offer → hire action triggers PA infotypes. Performance: goal setting (SMART) → continuous feedback → review (1–5 scale) → calibration → outcome feeds merit pay (IT0008). Onboarding "first 90 days": Day-1 ready, buddy program, training plan (LSO), 90-day review.

HCM integration (exam favourite)

Payroll results → FI (G/L, net pay to bank/AP). Labor cost → CO cost centers & CO-PA. MM employee purchase requests. PP work-center capacity ↔ headcount, shift schedules, labor routing times. Transfer an employee (IT0001) → the next payroll automatically routes salary to the new cost center.

Self-test

Q1What is an infotype, and which is the most critical?
A structured, time-dependent container for employee data. IT0001 (Org Assignment) is most critical — it links the employee to the FI cost center, so transfers reroute payroll automatically.
Q2Primary vs secondary (slash) wage types?
Primary (1000–9999) are entered in infotypes/time evaluation. Secondary "/" (/100–/999) are system-generated by the schema and must never be entered manually (e.g. /559 net pay).
Q3What does PT60 do?
Time Evaluation — converts raw time data (clock-in/out, absences, attendances) into payroll-relevant wage types/quota updates. The bridge between time management and payroll.
Q4What is retroactive payroll?
A past-dated change is auto-detected; SAP recalculates the affected prior periods in the next run and posts the difference — no manual adjustment (possible because infotypes keep full history).
Q5Give the HCM org hierarchy and say what runs per company code.
Client → Company Code → Personnel Area → Personnel Subarea → Employee Group & Subgroup. Payroll runs per company code.
Q6HCM vs SuccessFactors?
HCM = on-premise (ECC/S4), deep FI/CO/MM integration. SuccessFactors = SAP's cloud/SaaS platform (Employee Central, Recruiting, Learning, Performance), preferred for new implementations since 2015.
