Supply Chain — Materials Management (MM)

From purchase request to goods in the warehouse — the full Procure-to-Pay cycle, plus inventory, valuation, MRP, vendors and warehousing.

SAP MM covers four things: Procurement (requisition → payment), Inventory Management (stock levels & movements in real time), Material Planning (MRP: what to procure & when), and Valuation & Costing (standard or moving-average price).

Critical principle

Each step creates a linked document chain (PR → PO → GR → IR → Payment) — a complete audit trail. No step can be skipped or manually bypassed.

Purchase Requisition vs Purchase Order

Purchase Requisition (PR) ME51N

  • Internal request for a material/service
  • Created by any authorized user, or automatically by MRP
  • Release strategy: amount / material-group / cost-center based
  • No FI posting; CO records a requisition commitment (budget visibility)

Purchase Order (PO) ME21N

  • Formal, legally binding commitment to a vendor
  • From a PR, an info record, or a contract
  • Types: Standard, Subcontracting, Consignment, Stock Transport Order
  • CO posts a PO commitment; FI fires only on GR/IR
T-code pattern in MM

ME51N create PR → ME21N create PO → ME23N display PO. The last digit: 1 = Create, 2 = Change, 3 = Display; N = new UI.

Goods Receipt & the three-way match

At Goods Receipt (MIGO): stock quantity ↑, the GR/IR clearing account is debited (temporary), the Inventory G/L is credited (stock value enters FI in real time), and a Material Document is created.

Three-way match

Purchase Order (what was ordered & at what price) + Goods Receipt (what was actually received) + Vendor Invoice (what's being charged). If any two deviate beyond tolerance → invoice is automatically blocked. The GR/IR clearing account is temporary — it closes when the invoice is verified in MIRO.

Material valuation: standard vs moving average

Standard Price (S)

Fixed price per period (from CO product costing). All movements use it regardless of actual purchase price.

  • Enables variance analysis (actual vs standard)
  • Used in CO product costing & profitability
  • Mandatory for finished & semi-finished goods
  • Price differences → price difference account

Moving Average Price (V)

Recalculated after each goods receipt — weighted average of existing + incoming stock.

  • Reflects actual purchase costs automatically
  • No variance postings for normal receipts
  • Preferred for traded goods & raw materials with volatile prices
  • Harder to analyze trends; negative stock can distort it
Master-data trap

The valuation method lives in the material master (Accounting view) and cannot be changed once goods movements have occurred. Critical config decision.

MRP — Material Requirements Planning

MRP calculates what to procure or produce, in what quantity, and by when — automatically.

Inputs

  • Demand (sales orders / forecasts)
  • Current stock levels
  • Open purchase / production orders
  • Safety stock & lead times

Outputs

  • Planned orders → become production orders
  • Purchase requisitions → external procurement
  • Schedule lines (split against contracts)
  • Exception messages (reschedule in/out, expedite, cancel)

Vendor management & sourcing

Vendor Master XK01 / MK01

Three data areas: General (name, address, bank), FI/Company Code (payment terms, recon account, dunning), MM/Purchasing Org (currency, order ack, delivery tolerance).

XK01 creates all views · MK01 purchasing only · FK01 FI only · XK02 change.

Vendor Evaluation ME61

Scores vendors 1–100: Price 30% · Quality 25% · Delivery reliability 25% · Service 20%. Threshold for preferred vendor ≈ 80+. Scores feed automatic source determination.

Sourcing strategies: Single (one vendor — max leverage, max risk) · Dual (two — balance cost vs continuity) · Multi (3+ via RFQ ME41) · Preferred vendor via info record ME11. Group contract = ME31K.

MM integration — the golden rule

Golden rule of MM integration

Every material movement automatically generates the corresponding FI and CO document. There is no manual reconciliation between operations and finance — the document is created once and all downstream systems consume it.

→ FI: GR → inventory G/L debit; invoice → AP; GR/IR clearing each step; stock value on the balance sheet.
→ CO: PR/PO → commitment; goods issue to production → cost-center charge; procurement variance.
→ PP: MRP driven by production plan; goods issue (261) supplies production orders; BOM/routing → demand; backflushing.
→ SD: goods issue (601) → revenue/COGS; ATP queries MM stock; returns reverse the goods receipt.

Self-test

Q1Give the P2P document chain with T-codes.
PR (ME51N) → PO (ME21N) → GR (MIGO) → Invoice (MIRO) → Payment (F110).
Q2At what step does FI first post in P2P, and what is the GR/IR account?
FI fires at Goods Receipt (PR/PO only create CO commitments). GR/IR is a temporary clearing account debited at GR and closed when the invoice is verified in MIRO.
Q3What do movement types 101, 261 and 601 mean?
101 = GR for PO (stock ↑). 261 = GI for production (cost center charged). 601 = GI for delivery to customer (COGS debited).
Q4Standard vs moving average price — which is mandatory for finished goods?
Standard (S) — fixed, enables variance analysis, mandatory for finished/semi-finished goods. Moving average (V) — recalculated each GR, for raw/traded goods with volatile prices. Can't be changed after movements occur.
Q5List MRP inputs and outputs.
Inputs: demand, current stock, open POs/production orders, safety stock & lead times. Outputs: planned orders, purchase requisitions, schedule lines, exception messages.
Q6What are the vendor evaluation criteria and weights?
Price 30%, Quality 25%, Delivery reliability 25%, Service 20%. Preferred-vendor threshold ≈ 80+.
Q7IM vs WM — what's the difference?
IM tracks total stock at plant/storage-location level (no bins). WM manages physical stock at bin level using transfer orders. EWM adds advanced features (waves, slotting, yard).
