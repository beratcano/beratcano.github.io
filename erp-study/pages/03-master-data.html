Master Data & Data Governance

The quality of an ERP system is directly proportional to the quality of the data inside it. The whole chapter is one idea: garbage in, garbage out.

What is master data?

Definition

Core data shared across multiple business units or systems, relatively stable over time, and critical to operations.

AttributeMaster dataTransaction data
DefinitionDescribes core business entitiesRecords daily business operations
Change frequencyRarely changesChanges continuously
ExamplesCustomer, Product, Vendor, AccountOrder, Invoice, Payment, Stock movement
ScopeAll modules & systemsRelevant process / module only

A customer record is master data because it's used across hundreds of order transactions; the order itself is transaction data. If master data is flawed, all transaction data built on it is flawed.

Master data types in ERP

Customer Master SD · FI · CRM

  • Customer number & name
  • Address & contact
  • Payment terms & credit limit
  • Sales area & distribution channel
  • Account group assignment

Vendor Master MM · FI · SCM

  • Vendor number & name
  • Bank & payment details
  • Delivery lead times
  • Evaluation score
  • Tax identification

Material Master MM · PP · WM · FI

  • Material number & description
  • Unit of measure, weight, dimensions
  • Storage & purchasing data
  • Accounting & costing data
  • MRP planning parameters

Finance Master FI · CO · PS

  • Chart of Accounts structure
  • Cost center hierarchy
  • Profit center organization
  • Currency & exchange data
  • Reporting categories

Material master: views in SAP (the most complex master record!)

The material master holds "views" — each maintained by a different department for its own needs. A missing/incorrect view directly breaks that unit's processes.

Data quality: the 6 dimensions

Accuracy

Does the data correctly reflect the real world? Is the customer's address current?

Completeness

Are all required fields populated? Does the vendor record include payment details?

Consistency

Same data consistent across systems? Does the SAP customer name match CRM?

Timeliness

Updated when changes occur? Has the price-list expiry passed?

Uniqueness

Any duplicates for the same entity? Same customer under two codes?

Validity

Conforms to defined rules/formats? Is the UoM in the allowed values?

Mnemonic

"A C C T U V" → Accuracy, Completeness, Consistency, Timeliness, Uniqueness, Validity.

Data governance

Definition

The overall management of the availability, usability, integrity, and security of data through defined authorities, responsibilities, policies and standards.

Four pillars:

Policy & Standards
data definitions, naming conventions, mandatory fields, quality thresholds
Organization & Roles
data owner, data steward, data-quality team, governance committee
Processes & Workflows
creation/approval, change management, periodic cleansing, exception handling
Technology & Tools
MDM platform, data-quality tools, reporting dashboards, audit trails

Key roles: Owner vs Steward

RoleWhoResponsibilities
Data OwnerSenior / mid-level managerApproves policies & standards, sets quality targets/KPIs, authorizes access, accountable for data-related business outcomes, drives corrective action
Data StewardOperational expert in the business unitMonitors daily quality, reviews/approves new record requests, fixes/deduplicates records, trains users on entry standards
Technical Data StewardIT / ERP supportManages data model & integration rules, defines system validations, runs migration, develops the MDM platform
Don't mix these up

Owner = accountable (policy & outcomes, a manager). Steward = operational (daily quality, a business expert). Technical steward = IT (model, validations, migration).

Where it bites

  • Wrong vendor record → delayed/duplicate payments
  • Wrong address → shipment cancellations
  • Incomplete MRP data → production plan failure
  • Wrong price → revenue leakage; wrong cost center → bad reporting
  • Duplicate CRM records → inconsistent communication

Two real cases

Retail chaos (failure): 500-store chain migrated to SAP without cleansing — 3 vendor records for one supplier, 12,000 wrong units of measure, 800 customers missing tax numbers, 40% stock inconsistency → 6-month, €1.2M remediation. Lesson: governance & stewardship must exist before the project.

Global manufacturer (success): 18 countries, 8 months of governance before S/4HANA go-live; appointed data owners, trained 180 stewards, centralized MDM, cleaned 2.4M material records → 98.7% completeness, procurement −34%, invoice errors 12%→1.4%. Key: CEO sponsorship, business owned the data, governance launched 8 months early.

Remember

"Data quality is not an IT problem." KPIs calculated on bad data lead to wrong market decisions.

Why "block", not "delete"?

If records were deleted, historical transaction data would become meaningless. Instead records are blocked / deactivated to preserve the audit trail.

Critical success factor

Data migration is a business process, not a technical one. Without active involvement from business units, success is impossible.

Self-test

Q1List the six data-quality dimensions.
Accuracy, Completeness, Consistency, Timeliness, Uniqueness, Validity.
Q2Data Owner vs Data Steward — who does what?
Owner = senior manager, approves policy/standards, sets quality KPIs, authorizes access, accountable for outcomes. Steward = operational business expert, monitors daily quality, approves/fixes records, trains users. (Technical steward = IT: model, validations, migration, MDM platform.)
Q3Name the three MDM strategies and the best fit for each.
Centralized (single source of truth) — large multi-system enterprises. Decentralized (own data + sync) — mid-sized with diverse IT. Federated (local data + shared rules) — holdings/multinationals.
Q4Why are ERP records blocked instead of deleted?
To preserve the audit trail — deleting master records would make historical transaction data meaningless.
Q5Which material master view does each of Finance, Procurement and PP own?
Finance → Accounting view (valuation). Procurement → Purchasing view. PP → MRP/Planning view. (Plus Basic Data, Warehouse, Sales.)
Q6Roughly what share of project budget is data migration, and is it technical or business work?
15–25% of the budget; it is a business process, not a technical one — needs business-unit involvement.
