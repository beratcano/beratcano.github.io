Production Planning & Sales / Distribution

Manufacturing meets the market: how PP drives the shop floor, how SD runs Order-to-Cash, and how customer demand instantly becomes production.

SAP PP — Production Planning

PP manages the manufacturing process from demand to finished goods: SOP → Demand Management (PIR) → MRP → Production Execution. Org levels: Client → Company Code → Plant → Storage Location → Work Center.

PP master data: BOM, Routing, Work Center

Three objects define how a product is made — without them, no production order can be created.

Status flow (memorize the order)

CRTD (Created) → REL (Released) → PCNF (Partially Confirmed) → CNF (Confirmed) → DLV (Delivered) → TECO (Technically Completed) → CLSD (Closed). You can't confirm before release; you can't close before delivery.

MRP → PP integration & CRP

MRP (MD01/MD02) bridges demand to the shop floor: net requirement calc, lot sizing, lead-time scheduling, availability check, exception messages → outputs planned orders, purchase requisitions and exception messages. Planning horizon: the firm zone (within lead time) is frozen; the planning zone allows system proposals.

Capacity Requirements Planning (CRP)

CRP checks whether enough capacity exists. Available capacity = shifts × hours/shift × efficiency%. If load > available → overload flagged; the planner must reschedule, split, or add capacity (leveling via CM21; load CM01; overload CM04).

SAP SD — Sales & Distribution

SD manages the customer-facing process from quotation to cash. Org levels: Client → Company Code → Sales Organization → Distribution Channel → Division → Sales Office → Sales Group → Shipping Point.

SD master data & pricing

Customer Master

XD01 all views · VD01 SD only · FD01 FI only. General data, company-code data (payment terms, credit limit), sales-area data.

Material Master (SD views)

MM01. Needs SD views per Sales Org + Distribution Channel (delivering plant, tax classification, availability check).

Pricing — Condition Technique

VK11. Condition records (price, discount, surcharge, freight); an access sequence finds the right record by priority. Net value flows into the billing doc & FI.

Sales order processing (VA01) — four automatic checks

Pricing determination — condition technique finds price/discount/tax by customer-material.
ATP (Availability check) — confirms quantity & delivery date against stock, open POs, production orders.
Credit check (FI) — verifies credit limit; if exceeded the order is blocked (credit hold).
Transfer of requirements — passes demand to MRP; if ATP can't confirm, MRP may trigger production.

Order structure: Header (sold-to, dates, pricing procedure, terms) · Item (material, qty, confirmed qty, plant) · Schedule lines (confirmed dates/quantities) · Partner functions (Sold-to, Ship-to, Bill-to, Payer — each can differ).

Delivery & Goods Issue (the COGS trigger)

Outbound delivery (VL01N): create delivery → pick (WM transfer order) → pack/ship → Post Goods Issue (movement type 601).

Goods Issue = revenue recognition trigger

At GI (601): Cost of Goods Sold debited (P&L expense), Inventory credited (stock ↓ on the balance sheet). In manufacturing this is the moment delivery is confirmed and COGS must be recognized — regardless of when the invoice is raised.

Billing & revenue (VF01)

Billing creates the invoice and the FI revenue posting in one transaction: AR debited, Revenue credited, Tax payable credited — and a CO-PA document capturing revenue, COGS & margin per customer/product/region.

Credit management & ATP

Credit management (protects cash)

  • Credit limit per customer (per credit control area)
  • Exposure = open orders + deliveries + invoices + AR
  • Static check: exposure > limit → blocked
  • Dynamic check: adds future-horizon orders (more conservative)
  • Release blocked order: VKM3/VKM4

ATP (protects delivery promises)

ATP qty = on-hand stock + planned receipts − confirmed sales-order requirements (by date).

  • Full availability → full confirmation
  • Partial → confirm what's available, backlog rest
  • Unconfirmed lines → production requirement to MRP
  • Global ATP = cross-plant, needs APO/SCM

PP ↔ SD integration & the MM → PP → SD flow

Key insight

When a sales-order quantity exceeds ATP stock, SAP automatically creates a production requirement; MRP converts it to a planned order within minutes. Finished-goods receipts in PP re-trigger ATP for waiting customer orders. The module boundary is invisible to the business.

Self-test

Q1Name the three PP master-data objects and their role.
BOM (components — what), Routing (operations & times — how), Work Center (where; capacity, linked to CO cost center). Without all three, no production order can be created.
Q2Give the production order status flow.
CRTD → REL → PCNF → CNF → DLV → TECO → CLSD. (Can't confirm before release; can't close before delivery.)
Q3What are the four automatic checks at sales-order entry (VA01)?
Pricing determination, ATP availability check, credit check (FI), transfer of requirements to MRP.
Q4Why is Goods Issue (601) so important in O2C?
It's the revenue-recognition trigger: COGS is debited and inventory credited (stock ↓). Confirms delivery occurred, independent of the invoice.
Q5State the ATP formula.
ATP quantity = on-hand stock + planned receipts − confirmed sales-order requirements (by date).
Q6Static vs dynamic credit check?
Static: total exposure (open orders+deliveries+invoices+AR) > limit → block. Dynamic: also adds open orders within a future horizon — more conservative.
Q7What FI postings does billing (VF01) create?
AR debited, Revenue credited, Tax payable credited — plus a CO-PA document (revenue, COGS, margin by customer/product/region).
