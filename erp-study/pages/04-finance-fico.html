Finance & Accounting — FI / CO

External reporting (FI) and internal performance management (CO) — and how every operational transaction in ERP creates an automatic financial consequence.

FI in one breath

FI records every financial event and produces statutory reports for external stakeholders (shareholders, tax authorities, regulators, banks). The four verbs: Record → Classify → Report → Comply.

Organizational levels in SAP FI

ClientHighest level = the whole company group. Master data here is shared across all company codes.
Company CodeThe legally independent entity — own balance sheet & P&L. Most FI postings happen here.
Business AreaOptional segment for internal reporting across company codes (segment balance sheets without separate legal entities).
Fiscal Year / Posting PeriodDefines the accounting year & which periods are open — the time dimension of all transactions.

Multinational group: 1 Client → many Company Codes (DE, TR, US) → each with its own Chart of Accounts & fiscal year variant.

Chart of Accounts (CoA)

The complete list of General Ledger (G/L) accounts used to classify every financial transaction.

Operating CoA
Day-to-day postings; assigned to company codes. Most postings happen here.
Group CoA
Consolidated reporting across the group; maps from operating accounts.
Country-specific CoA
Local statutory reporting; runs parallel to the operating CoA.

Mnemonic

1-2-3-4-5 = A-L-E-R-E: Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenue, Expense.

General Ledger & document types

The G/L is the central repository for all financial data. Every transaction creates an FI document with at least two line items that must balance — debit = credit (double-entry bookkeeping).

Memory hook for doc types

D = customer (Debitor): DR invoice, DZ payment. K = vendor (Kreditor): KR invoice, KZ payment. R=Rechnung (invoice), Z=Zahlung (payment).

Three-way match — the core AP control

Before an invoice posts, SAP verifies three documents match within tolerance: ① Purchase Order (what was ordered & price) · ② Goods Receipt (what was received) · ③ Vendor Invoice (what's being charged). If quantities/prices deviate beyond tolerance → invoice is blocked automatically.

Other AP capabilities: automatic payment program F110 (schedules/executes by due date), payment-method config (transfer, check, SEPA), dunning & overdue tracking, real-time G/L update.

Controlling (CO): internal management

FI — Financial Accounting

  • Legally mandated external reports
  • Balance sheet, P&L, cash flow
  • Follows GAAP / IFRS
  • Visible to shareholders & auditors
  • Closed periods — no retrospective changes

CO — Controlling

  • Internal management decision support
  • Cost analysis, profitability, budgeting
  • No external standard — per company need
  • Data internal, not disclosed
  • Flexible — allocations can be re-run

Cost centers, profit centers & internal orders

ObjectWhat it isKey trait

Product costing & profitability analysis

Product Costing (CO-PC)

Calculates the standard cost of a manufactured product using Bill of Materials + routing from PP. (Illustrative split: material ~48%, labor ~22%, machine/OH ~18%, overhead ~12%.)

Profitability Analysis (CO-PA)

Analyzes profitability across multiple dimensions simultaneously: customer segment, product group, region, distribution channel, sales rep. The bridge from operations to strategic P&L.

FI/CO integration & real-time posting

Key principle

In SAP, financial postings are generated automatically by operational transactions — not by manual journal entry. This eliminates the reconciliation gap between operational and financial data.

Every operational step triggers a simultaneous financial update — no batch jobs:

Purchase Requisition ME51NNo FI postingCommitment recorded

The accounting equation, enforced

SAP won't save a document if debits ≠ credits. Every FI document must satisfy Assets = Liabilities + Equity — unbalanced books are impossible.

Critical FI/CO config decisions

Made once at project start, very hard to change later:

  • Fiscal Year Variant (HIGH) — calendar vs non-calendar; number of posting periods (typically 12 + 4 special).
  • Chart of Accounts (HIGH) — account ranges; adding accounts later is possible, restructuring is not.
  • Controlling Area (HIGH) — one company code or several; affects cross-company allocations & group CO-PA.
  • Parallel Ledger Strategy (MED) — must be configured before any transaction posts.
  • Cost Center Hierarchy & Profit Center Structure (MED) — CO-PA dimensions are fixed at config; can't be added retroactively.
Case proof

Turkcell consolidated 14 countries on one S/4HANA instance with a group CoA + parallel ledgers (IFRS leading + local GAAP) → consolidation cut from 4 weeks to 4 days, automated FX revaluation, near-zero intercompany errors.

Self-test

Q1Which FI org level is the legally independent entity, and what does it own?
The Company Code — it has its own balance sheet and P&L, and is where most FI postings occur. (Client > Company Code > Business Area > Fiscal Year/Period.)
Q2What are the three documents in the three-way match?
Purchase Order + Goods Receipt + Vendor Invoice. If they deviate beyond tolerance, the invoice is auto-blocked.
Q3What do G/L ranges 1–5 represent?
1 Assets, 2 Liabilities, 3 Equity, 4 Revenue, 5 Expense.
Q4Cost center vs profit center vs internal order?
Cost center — incurs costs, permanent. Profit center — revenue + costs, internal P&L per segment. Internal order — collects costs for a time-limited activity; lifecycle open→active→settled→closed.
Q5FI vs CO — external/internal and which one follows IFRS?
FI = external, statutory, follows GAAP/IFRS, closed periods. CO = internal, no external standard, flexible (allocations can re-run).
Q6Name the FI document types for customer/vendor invoices and payments.
Customer invoice DR, customer payment DZ; vendor invoice KR, vendor payment KZ; manual G/L SA; asset AA.
Q7What does CO-PA do that a profit center doesn't?
CO-PA analyzes profitability across multiple dimensions simultaneously (customer, product, region, channel, rep) — the bridge from billing to strategic P&L.
