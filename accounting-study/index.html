The whole course in three moves

Everything you'll be tested on fits one of these.

① Classify costs

Sort every cost: product vs period, direct vs indirect, DM / DL / MOH, prime vs conversion. Get this right and everything else follows.

② Report the cost

Build the schedule of cost of goods manufactured and the income statement, then a unit cost. Trace cost through RM → WIP → FG.

③ Decide with cost

Use relevant costs (ignore sunk costs!) for special orders, make-or-buy, constraints and equipment replacement.

Exam-night plan

Lock down cost classification (Ch 2) — the most common quick-marks question. Drill the COGM → income statement format (Ch 3) and the job-order journal entries + overhead variance (Ch 5) until automatic. Be ready to flip a statement into contribution format (Ch 4) and to run the direct vs step-down allocation (Ch 6). Finally rehearse the relevant-cost decision rules (Ch 7–8): special order, make-or-buy, constraints, equipment replacement. Finish on Exam Practice.
