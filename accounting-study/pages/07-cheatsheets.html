Cost classification at a glance

DimensionCategories
By functionProduct (inventoriable: DM + DL + MOH) vs Period (selling & admin, expensed now)
By traceabilityDirect (traced: DM, DL) vs Indirect (allocated: MOH)
Product cost partsDM (direct materials) · DL (direct labor) · MOH (indirect materials + indirect labor + factory rent/utilities/insurance/depreciation)
CombinationsPrime = DM + DL · Conversion = DL + MOH
Fast classification test

Incurred inside the factory to make the product? → product cost. Selling, delivery to customers, or office/admin? → period cost. Packaging the customer buys = product; packing to ship = period.

Memory hook

Same shape three times: Beginning + Added − Ending. RM adds purchases, WIP adds DM+DL+MOH, FG adds COGM. Order: DM → mfg costs → COGM → COGS → income statement.

Relevant-cost decision rules

DecisionRule / what's relevant
Special order (spare capacity)Accept if price > incremental variable cost. Ignore fixed costs & full unit cost.
Make-or-buyCompare relevant cost to make (variable + avoidable fixed + opportunity cost) vs cost to buy.
Constraint / product mixMaximise contribution margin per unit of the constraining resource (e.g. per machine hour). Multiple constraints → linear programming.
Drop a customer/segmentDrop only if avoidable costs > lost revenue. Sunk depreciation is irrelevant.
Add a customerNew equipment cost (future depreciation) IS relevant.
Equipment replacementRelevant: old machine's current disposal value & new machine's cost. Irrelevant: book value & accumulated depreciation (sunk), and any accounting gain/loss.

Key definitions

TermMeaning
Relevant costExpected future cost that differs between alternatives
Sunk costPast, unavoidable cost — never relevant (e.g. book value)
Opportunity costProfit forgone by not using a resource in its next-best use; not recorded in the books
Differential cost/revenueThe difference in total cost/revenue between two alternatives
Incremental cost/revenueThe additional total cost/revenue from an activity
Contribution marginSales price − variable cost (per unit or in total)
Throughput margin (TOC)TOC measure to maximise while cutting investments & operating costs; manage the bottleneck
Quantitative vs qualitativeMeasurable in numbers (financial/non-financial) vs not measurable in numbers

Relevant test

Future + Differs = Relevant. Same in both options or already spent → cross it out.

Prime vs conversion

Prime = DM + DL; Conversion = DL + MOH. DL is the overlap.
