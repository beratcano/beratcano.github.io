Job-Order Costing: Flow of Costs & Journal Entries

When a company makes distinct, identifiable jobs, cost is tracked per job via journal entries. Master the five accounts, the nine entries, and the overhead account that trips everyone up.

What problem are we solving?

Earlier (COGM) we treated the whole factory as one bucket of totals. Job-order costing is used when products are distinct jobs (a print shop printing 5,000 books for Client A, then 200 invitations for Client B). Each job is unique, so we record the journey of cost — buy → use → finish → sell — with debits and credits.

The debit/credit logic

One rule: assets & expenses go UP with a DEBIT, DOWN with a CREDIT. All five accounts behave this way. Every entry is one of two flavors:

(A) Moving cost along the map

Cost leaves Room A (credit it, ↓) and enters Room B (debit it, ↑).

Materials to the floor: Dr WIP / Cr Raw Materials Inventory.

(B) Bringing cost in from outside

Debit the room it lands in; credit the source (Cash / A/P / Accum. Depr. / Wages Payable…).

Buy materials on account: Dr Raw Materials Inv / Cr Accounts Payable.

The Factory Overhead Control account (the tricky one)

DM and DL are traceable → straight into WIP. Overhead is indirect, arrives from many sources, and its true total isn't known until year-end. So we collect all indirect costs in one bucket, then push a clean estimated amount to WIP.

DEBIT side = ACTUAL (in)CREDIT side = APPLIED (out)
indirect materials, indirect labor, rent, utilities, depreciation, insurance, taxesapplied to WIP (= predetermined rate × actual activity)

Memory hook

FOH Control is debited many times (each actual cost) and credited once (the single applying entry: Dr WIP / Cr FOH Control).

The leftover: under/overapplied variance

Actual and Applied almost never match — the leftover balance is the variance. The collector is temporary, so it must end at zero: close it to COGS.

CaseClosing entryEffect on COGS
Actual > Applied → UnderappliedDr COGS / Cr FOH ControlCOGS goes up (add)

Remember

Underapplied → add to COGS (jobs were charged too little). Overapplied → subtract from COGS.

The 4 traps
  1. Entries 2 & 3 split: direct → WIP, indirect → FOH Control. Office/selling salaries are a third path → period expense (neither WIP nor FOH).
  2. Entry 5 is applied (rate × actual driver), never actual.
  3. A sale = two entries (7 revenue + 8 cost). Don't drop the cost side.
  4. Splits inside one entry — e.g. insurance 90% factory / 10% office → factory part to FOH Control, office part to expense.

Applying overhead (predetermined rate)

The two formulas

Predetermined OH Rate = Estimated Overhead ÷ Estimated Cost Driver
Applied Overhead = Predetermined OH Rate × Actual Cost Driver

The cost driver can be machine hours, direct labor hours, or direct labor cost (a %):

  • Smart Touch: rate = 40% of DL cost → 169,000 × 40% = 67,600 applied.
  • Almeda: rate = 175% of DL cost → 200,000 × 175% = 350,000 applied.
  • Departmental rates (e.g. Cavitt): compute a separate rate per department and apply each department's rate to its own activity — never blend departments.

After the entries: the statements

COGM (same as Ch 3)

DM Used + Direct Labor + Applied FOH = manufacturing cost; + Beg WIP − End WIP = COGM.

COGS (with variance)

Beg FG + COGM − End FG = COGS (before variance); ± overhead variance (under = +, over = −) = adjusted COGS.

"Using applied overhead" → build with applied OH, then adjust COGS for the variance. "Using actual overhead" → fold actual overhead straight in (no separate variance step). Work the full Farid, Pineapple, Almeda, Cavitt, Don & Constance problems in the Practice chapter.

Self-test

Q1Name the five accounts costs flow through.
Raw Materials Inventory → Work-in-Process → Finished Goods → Cost of Goods Sold, with Factory Overhead Control feeding WIP.
Q2Which side of FOH Control is actual, which is applied?
Debit = actual (collected, many times). Credit = applied to WIP (once, = rate × actual driver).
Q3Underapplied vs overapplied — how do you close each to COGS?
Underapplied (actual > applied): Dr COGS / Cr FOH → adds to COGS. Overapplied (actual < applied): Dr FOH / Cr COGS → subtracts from COGS.
Q4Write the predetermined rate and applied overhead formulas.
Rate = Estimated Overhead ÷ Estimated Cost Driver. Applied = Rate × Actual Cost Driver.
Q5When materials are issued, where do the direct and indirect portions go?
Direct materials → WIP; indirect materials → FOH Control; both credit Raw Materials Inventory.
