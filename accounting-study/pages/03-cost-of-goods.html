Chapter 3 · The manufacturing statements

Cost of Goods Manufactured & the Income Statement

The format that earns the big marks. Learn the cost flow, the COGM schedule and the manufacturing income statement until you can reproduce them from memory.

The flow of costs through inventory

Manufacturing costs flow through the three inventories and finally hit the income statement:

Raw MaterialsDM purchased & used
→
Work-in-ProcessDM + DL + MOH added
→
Finished Goodscompleted (= COGM)
→
Cost of Goods Soldwhen sold (income statement)

The three formulas you must know

① Direct materials used

Beg. RM inventory
+ Purchases of DM (+ freight-in)
− End. RM inventory
= Direct materials used

② Cost of goods manufactured

Beg. WIP inventory
+ DM used + DL + MOH (= total mfg costs)
− End. WIP inventory
= Cost of goods manufactured

③ Cost of goods sold

Beg. FG inventory
+ Cost of goods manufactured
− End. FG inventory
= Cost of goods sold

Memory hook — same shape three times

Every step is "Beginning + Added − Ending = Used/Out." RM uses purchases, WIP adds DM+DL+MOH, FG adds COGM. Learn the pattern once, apply it three times.

Then the income statement

Net Sales RevenueSales − sales discounts/returns
− Cost of Goods Soldfrom formula ③
= Gross profit (gross margin)Net Sales − COGS
− Selling & administrative expensesthe period costs (marketing, distribution, customer service, admin)
= Operating incomethe bottom line
Unit product cost

Cost per unit = Cost of Goods Manufactured ÷ total units produced. Managers use it to set prices and to value the units sold. (Service company → cost per service; merchandising company → cost per item.)

Order of operations

Always go DM used → total mfg costs → COGM → COGS → income statement. COGS needs COGM (which needs total mfg costs, which needs DM used). Do them in that order and you can't get lost.

Watch-outs (they hide these in problems)

  • Freight-in / transportation on direct materials is added to DM purchases (it's a cost of acquiring materials). Discounts on materials are subtracted.
  • Plant supplies are indirect materials → MOH. If beginning/ending plant-supply inventories are given, compute supplies used the same "Beg + purchases − ending" way.
  • Sales discounts / returns reduce sales to get net sales before subtracting COGS.
  • Marketing, distribution, customer-service, and G&A costs are period costs — they go below gross margin, never inside COGM.

Self-test

Q1Write the formula for direct materials used.
Beginning RM + Purchases (+ freight-in − discounts) − Ending RM = Direct materials used.
Q2How do you get from total manufacturing costs to cost of goods manufactured?
Add beginning WIP and subtract ending WIP: COGM = Beg WIP + (DM used + DL + MOH) − End WIP.
Q3How do you get cost of goods sold from COGM?
COGS = Beginning FG + COGM − Ending FG.
Q4Where does freight-in on raw materials go — and where does delivery to customers go?
Freight-in on materials → added to DM purchases (product cost). Delivery to customers → a period (selling) cost, below gross margin.
Q5How is unit product cost calculated?
Cost of goods manufactured ÷ total units produced.
