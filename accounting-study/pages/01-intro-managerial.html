Introduction to Managerial Accounting

What managerial accounting is, how it differs from financial accounting, and the three kinds of company whose costs you'll be analysing all course long.

Financial vs managerial accounting

Financial accounting

  • Produces financial statements
  • For external users: investors, creditors, government authorities
  • Reports on the company as a whole
  • Governed by external rules; historical

Managerial accounting

  • Generates reports for planning — one planning tool is the budget
  • Controlling: evaluating the plan by comparing actual results to the budget
  • For internal managers; future-oriented
  • Weighing costs against benefits = cost/benefit analysis
Memory hook

Financial = For outsiders, Facts of the past. Managerial = for Managers, planning the future (budget) then controlling (actual vs budget).

Management accountability & stakeholders

Management accountability is the manager's responsibility to the organization's stakeholders to wisely manage its resources. Stakeholders have an interest in the business and include:

Customers
Creditors
Suppliers
Investors

Acting ethically toward these stakeholders is part of management accountability — managers are expected to follow professional ethical standards.

Today's business environment

Forces shaping it

  • Shift toward a service economy
  • Global competition
  • Time-based competition

Time-based competition tools

  • ERP systems integrate a company's data
  • E-commerce sells to customers worldwide
  • Just-in-Time (JIT) — an inventory management tool

Total Quality Management (TQM)

A philosophy of continuous improvement in products and processes; creates a culture of cooperation. Each step adds value to the end product — this is the value chain.

Triple bottom line

The economic, social & environmental impact of doing business — the three P's: Profits, People, Planet.

Memory hook

Triple bottom line = 3 P's: Profits · People · Planet. TQM → continuous improvement → value chain.

→

Why this matters

These three inventories are the backbone of the cost of goods manufactured schedule in Chapter 3. A manufacturer's cost flows RM → WIP → FG → Cost of Goods Sold.

Self-test

Q1Who uses financial vs managerial accounting, and what is "controlling"?
Financial → external users (investors, creditors, government). Managerial → internal managers. Controlling = evaluating the plan by comparing actual results to the budget.
Q2What is management accountability and who are the stakeholders?
The manager's responsibility to stakeholders to wisely manage the organization's resources. Stakeholders: customers, creditors, suppliers, investors.
Q3How are costs treated in a service company?
All of a service company's costs are period costs, expensed in the period incurred (they have no inventory of products).
Q4Name the three manufacturing inventories in order of flow.
Raw Materials (RM) → Work-in-Process (WIP) → Finished Goods (FG).
Q5What is the triple bottom line?
The economic, social and environmental impact of business — Profits, People, Planet.
