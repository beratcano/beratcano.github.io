Chapter 6 · Overhead distribution

Service Department Cost Allocation

Service departments support the factory but never touch the product. Their cost can't vanish — it must be pushed onto the production departments. Two methods do it: Direct and Step-Down.

The setup

Production (producing) departments

Actually make the product (e.g. Assembly, Treating). Product cost is computed only here.

Service departments

Support the factory but never touch the product (e.g. Materials Handling, Repairs & Maintenance, Building & Grounds).

Goal

Push (allocate) all service-department cost onto the production departments, so every overhead dollar ends up where product cost is calculated.

The allocation base (the "pizza split")

Split cost by who used the service most — that measuring stick is the allocation base. Two steps:

Step 1 — Rate

= service department's cost ÷ total base (cost per "slice")

Step 2 — Share

= each dept's base amount × rate (slices × price)

Step-down method

Rule: put the service departments in an order; allocate one at a time, each spreading to everyone below it (remaining service depts + production). Two extra rules: (1) once allocated, a department is closed — never allocate back; (2) a department grows by what it receives before it spreads.

Mental model

Direct = service depts pour straight down into production (no sideways flow). Step-Down = a staircase — each service dept cascades onto the ones below; the last drop lands in production.

After allocation: production-department overhead rates

Final step

Once all service cost sits in the production departments, compute each one's rate (same idea as the predetermined rate): Production Dept OH Rate = Total Overhead in that Dept ÷ Dept's Activity (e.g. machine-hours).

Example (Lisa Lewis): divide each producing department's total overhead by its estimated machine-hours (Parts Fabrication 800 MH, Assembly 600 MH) to get a per-MH rate, then apply it to products. The full Lisa Lewis problem is in Practice.

Self-test

Q1In the direct method, what is the allocation base for each service department?
Only the production departments' usage — service-to-service usage is ignored.
Q2State the two extra rules of the step-down method.
(1) Once a department is allocated it's closed — never allocate back to it. (2) A department grows by cost received from earlier departments before it spreads its (bigger) total.
Q3What's the two-step allocation calculation?
Rate = service cost ÷ total base; each department's share = its base amount × rate. Shares must add back to the original cost.
Q4After allocation, how do you get a production department's overhead rate?
Total overhead in that department ÷ the department's activity (e.g. machine-hours).
Q5Does order matter in each method?
Direct: no. Step-down: yes — you must allocate in a chosen sequence.
